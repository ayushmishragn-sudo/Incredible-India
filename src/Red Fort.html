A Marvel of Mughal Architecture

History of Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, is a historic fortification and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The fort gets its name from the massive red sandstone walls that enclose the structure.

The Red Fort witnessed key moments in history, including the rise and fall of the Mughal Empire, British colonization, and India's independence struggle. It remains a symbol of India's rich heritage and sovereignty.

Architecture of the Red Fort

The Red Fort showcases the grandeur of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles. Its vast complex includes palaces, gardens, and public halls. Notable features include:

  • The Lahori Gate, the main entrance, which leads to a bustling market.
  • The Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience) and Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience) for royal assemblies.
  • The Rang Mahal, a palace adorned with beautiful artwork and fountains.
  • The Khas Mahal, a private residence of the emperor.

Visiting Information

The Red Fort is open to visitors every day except Mondays. A sound and light show is held in the evenings, narrating the history of the fort and its rulers. Tickets can be purchased at the entrance or online.

Key points to explore include the Lahori Gate, Chatta Chowk Bazaar, the museums, and the beautiful gardens. Guided tours are recommended to gain deeper insights into the fort's history and significance.
