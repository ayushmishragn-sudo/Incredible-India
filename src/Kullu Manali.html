History of Kullu Manali

Kullu and Manali are picturesque towns nestled in the Himachal Pradesh region of India, known as the "Valley of Gods." Kullu is famous for its rich cultural heritage and ancient temples, while Manali is celebrated for its snow-capped mountains and serene landscapes.

The region has been a prominent trade route in ancient times and is deeply intertwined with Indian mythology, with the Kullu Valley being associated with the Sage Manu, the creator of human civilization according to Hindu tradition.

Major Attractions in Kullu Manali

  • Rohtang Pass: A popular destination for adventure and breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
  • Solang Valley: Known for its winter sports like skiing and summer activities like paragliding.
  • Hidimba Devi Temple: A unique wooden temple surrounded by cedar forests in Manali.
  • Great Himalayan National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with diverse flora and fauna, perfect for trekking.
  • Beas River: A serene river ideal for white-water rafting and scenic views.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit Kullu Manali depends on your interests:

  • Summer (March to June): Ideal for sightseeing and adventure sports.
  • Winter (October to February): Perfect for snow lovers and skiing enthusiasts.

Accommodation ranges from luxury resorts to budget-friendly stays. Don’t forget to explore the local handicrafts and woolen items from Kullu’s famous markets.

Nearby attractions include Kasol, Manikaran Sahib, and Naggar Castle for a comprehensive Himachal experience.
