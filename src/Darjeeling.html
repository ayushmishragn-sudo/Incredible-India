Welcome to Darjeeling

The Queen of the Hills

History of Darjeeling

Darjeeling, located in the Indian state of West Bengal, was originally a part of the Sikkim kingdom and later annexed by the British in the 19th century. It became a popular summer retreat for the British colonial rulers.

The town is renowned for its colonial-era architecture, vibrant cultural heritage, and world-famous Darjeeling tea, which earned it a UNESCO World Heritage designation.

Major Attractions in Darjeeling

  • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway: Known as the "Toy Train," a UNESCO World Heritage Site offering scenic rides through the hills.
  • Tiger Hill: Famous for stunning sunrise views over Mount Kanchenjunga.
  • Tea Gardens: Explore lush tea estates producing the world-renowned Darjeeling tea.
  • Peace Pagoda: A serene Buddhist monument symbolizing harmony and tranquility.
  • Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park: Home to rare species like the red panda and snow leopard.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit Darjeeling is during the months of **March to May** and **October to December**, when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear.

How to Reach:

  • **By Air:** Bagdogra Airport is the nearest airport, located about 70 km away.
  • **By Train:** New Jalpaiguri is the nearest railway station, connected to major cities.
  • **By Road:** Well-connected by road from Siliguri and nearby towns.

Visitors can enjoy local attractions such as Tibetan markets, traditional food, and trekking routes around Singalila National Park.
