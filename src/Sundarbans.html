Welcome to Sundarbans

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the Largest Mangrove Forest

History of the Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, shared between India and Bangladesh, is the largest tidal halophytic mangrove forest in the world. It derives its name from the Sundari trees that dominate the region.

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Sundarbans is steeped in history and mythology, often associated with the tales of Bonbibi, the protector of the forest, and the stories of the Royal Bengal Tiger.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit the Sundarbans is from **November to February**, when the weather is pleasant, and wildlife sightings are frequent.

How to Explore:

  • **Boat Safaris:** The most popular way to explore the intricate waterways and spot wildlife.
  • **Observation Towers:** Located at Dobanki, Sajnekhali, and Sudhanyakhali for a closer view of animals.
  • **Eco-Villages:** Experience the local culture and lifestyle of the Sundarbans communities.

Visitors need permits, which can be obtained online or at entry points. Hiring a licensed guide is recommended for safety and a better experience.
