The Palace of Winds

History of Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. It was designed by Lal Chand Ustad in the form of a crown to resemble Lord Krishna's crown. The palace was constructed to allow royal women to observe everyday life and festivals on the street below without being seen, in accordance with the purdah system.

The Hawa Mahal stands as a symbol of Jaipur's rich cultural heritage and serves as an important landmark in the Pink City.

Architectural Marvel of Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is an extraordinary five-story structure made of red and pink sandstone. Its unique honeycomb-like facade consists of 953 intricately designed jharokhas (small windows). These windows allow cool air to circulate, providing natural ventilation even during the peak summer months.

  • The pyramid-shaped structure is adorned with floral designs, dome-shaped canopies, and latticework.
  • The palace's interiors are simple compared to its grand exterior and feature small rooms and corridors.
  • The design reflects a blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles.

Visiting Information

The Hawa Mahal is open to visitors every day from 9:00 AM to 4:30 PM. The entry fee is nominal, with separate charges for Indian and foreign tourists.

The best time to visit is early in the morning when the sunlight illuminates the palace, making it appear golden. Nearby attractions include the City Palace, Jantar Mantar, and Johari Bazaar for shopping.

Photography enthusiasts will find plenty of opportunities to capture the stunning architecture and scenic views of Jaipur from the top of the palace.
