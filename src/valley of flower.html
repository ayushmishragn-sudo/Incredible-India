Welcome to the Valley of Flowers

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a Blooming Wonderland

Flora of the Valley of Flowers

The Valley of Flowers is home to over 500 species of wildflowers, many of which are rare and endangered. The valley is a vibrant tapestry of colors, with blooms that range from orchids and poppies to primulas and rhododendrons.

Some of the notable species include:

  • Blue Poppy: A striking flower that is the signature bloom of the valley.
  • Rhododendron: Found in abundance, these flowers add a burst of red, pink, and white to the landscape.
  • Primula: Known for its beautiful colors, ranging from yellow to purple.
  • Marigold: Often seen in the meadows, adding a golden hue to the valley.
  • Wild Roses: Adding a delicate fragrance and pink tones to the scenery.

Trekking in the Valley of Flowers

The Valley of Flowers National Park offers several trekking routes that pass through scenic landscapes, rushing rivers, and dense forests. The trek to the valley is an adventure in itself, with a gradual ascent leading to the magical valley.

Popular Trekking Routes include:

  • Govindghat to Ghangaria: A 13 km trek that is the starting point for most visitors.
  • Ghangaria to Valley of Flowers: A 3.5 km trek that takes you deep into the heart of the valley.
  • Hemkund Sahib Trek: For those seeking a more challenging adventure, this trek connects to the famous Hemkund Sahib, a sacred site for Sikhs, and offers panoramic views of the valley.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit the Valley of Flowers is between **July and September**, during the monsoon and early autumn, when the flowers are in full bloom.

How to Reach:

  • **By Air:** The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, about 300 km away from the Valley.
  • **By Train:** Haridwar Railway Station is the closest major railway station, approximately 280 km from the valley.
  • **By Road:** From Haridwar or Rishikesh, buses or taxis can be taken to Govindghat, the base for the trek to the valley.

Visitors need to be prepared for the trek with proper gear and physical fitness. Entry to the Valley of Flowers is regulated, so a permit is required, which can be obtained at Govindghat or Ghangaria.
