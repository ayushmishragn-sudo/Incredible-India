History of Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala Backwaters, a network of lagoons, lakes, and canals parallel to the Arabian Sea, have been an integral part of Keralaâ€™s history and culture. These tranquil waterways have been used for centuries for trade and transportation, connecting the coastal areas with the inland regions.

Traditionally, the backwaters were home to vibrant village communities engaged in agriculture, fishing, and coir-making. Today, they are a major attraction for tourists seeking peace and natural beauty.

Major Attractions in Kerala Backwaters

  • Alleppey (Alappuzha): Known as the "Venice of the East," famous for houseboat cruises and lush landscapes.
  • Kumarakom: A charming village on Vembanad Lake, ideal for bird watching and backwater resorts.
  • Ashtamudi Lake: A picturesque lake offering houseboat tours and stunning sunsets.
  • Munroe Island: A serene spot for exploring narrow canals and village life.
  • Pathiramanal Island: A haven for bird lovers, accessible only by boat.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit the Kerala Backwaters is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for cruising. Monsoon months (June to September) also offer a unique charm with lush greenery and cool weather.

Houseboats, or "Kettuvallams," provide a luxurious way to explore the backwaters, complete with traditional Kerala cuisine and modern amenities. Visitors can also enjoy canoe rides for a closer look at village life and natural beauty.

Local attractions include spice markets, Ayurvedic centers, and Kathakali performances, giving a complete cultural experience of Kerala.
