History of Rishikesh

Rishikesh, located in the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, is a sacred city revered as the "Gateway to the Garhwal Himalayas." It holds great significance in Hinduism as a pilgrimage site and is believed to be blessed by the presence of Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva.

The city gained international fame in 1968 when The Beatles visited the ashram of Maharishi Mahesh Yogi to learn transcendental meditation. Since then, it has been a hub for spiritual seekers and yoga enthusiasts from around the globe.

Major Attractions in Rishikesh

  • Lakshman Jhula and Ram Jhula: Iconic suspension bridges over the Ganges, offering breathtaking views of the river and surrounding temples.
  • Triveni Ghat: A sacred ghat where evening Ganga Aarti is performed, attracting large crowds for its spiritual ambiance.
  • Parmarth Niketan: A renowned ashram offering yoga, meditation, and spiritual retreats.
  • Neer Garh Waterfall: A beautiful natural waterfall located amidst serene forests, perfect for trekking and relaxation.
  • Adventure Sports: Activities like white-water rafting, bungee jumping, and camping draw thrill-seekers to Rishikesh.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit Rishikesh is between October and March when the weather is pleasant. Yoga festivals and spiritual events are commonly held during this period.

Visitors are advised to explore the local vegetarian cuisine and observe the city's strict no-alcohol policy, which aligns with its spiritual ethos.

Accommodations range from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious retreats. Nearby attractions like Haridwar and the Rajaji National Park make Rishikesh an excellent base for exploring Uttarakhand.
