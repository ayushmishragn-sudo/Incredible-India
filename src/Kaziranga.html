Welcome to Kaziranga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Assam

History of Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park, located in Assam, was established in 1905 as a protected area for the Indian one-horned rhinoceros. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1985, it spans over 430 square kilometers of grasslands, wetlands, and forests.

The park owes its conservation success to dedicated efforts by British officials, Indian conservationists, and the local Karbi and Mising tribes.

Wildlife and Biodiversity

  • One-Horned Rhinoceros: Home to two-thirds of the world’s population of this iconic species.
  • Asian Elephants: Large herds of these majestic animals roam freely in the park.
  • Bengal Tigers: Kaziranga is a designated Tiger Reserve with a healthy population of this apex predator.
  • Birds: A paradise for birdwatchers, with over 480 species including pelicans, storks, and eagles.
  • Other Species: Swamp deer, wild buffalo, and various reptiles, including the Indian rock python.

Visiting Information

The park is open to visitors from **November to April**, with the best time to visit being between **February and March** for pleasant weather and wildlife sightings.

Safari Options:

  • **Jeep Safari:** Covers extensive areas of the park and offers excellent opportunities to spot wildlife.
  • **Elephant Safari:** A unique experience to get closer to animals, especially rhinos.

Entry permits are required and can be obtained at the park’s gates or online. Guided tours are recommended for a safe and informative visit.

Nearby attractions include the tea gardens of Assam, Majuli Island, and cultural performances of the Bihu dance.
