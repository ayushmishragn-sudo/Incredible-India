History of Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the grandest royal residences in India. Built in the Indo-Saracenic architectural style, it was designed by British architect Henry Irwin in the early 20th century.

The palace served as the residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and has witnessed centuries of royal history, cultural festivals, and political milestones. It is now one of the most visited tourist attractions in India.

Major Attractions in Mysore Palace

  • The Durbar Hall: Known for its ornate ceilings and beautiful chandeliers.
  • The Ambavilasa: A luxurious hall used for private meetings by the royal family.
  • Illuminations: The palace is lit with over 100,000 bulbs on weekends and public holidays.
  • Dasara Festival: A grand celebration showcasing Mysore’s rich cultural heritage.
  • The Museum: Displays artifacts, royal costumes, and paintings of the Wadiyar era.

Visiting Information

The Mysore Palace is open daily from 10:00 AM to 5:30 PM. The best time to visit is during the Dasara Festival (September-October) to experience its vibrant cultural essence.

Entry fees for Indian nationals are affordable, while foreign visitors are charged a nominal fee. Photography is allowed in the palace premises but prohibited inside certain sections.

Nearby attractions include Chamundi Hills, St. Philomena’s Church, and Brindavan Gardens, offering a complete Mysore experience.
