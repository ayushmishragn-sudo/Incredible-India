Welcome to Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a Treasure Trove of History

History of Hampi

Hampi, located in the Indian state of Karnataka, was once the capital of the mighty Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century. Known for its grandeur and prosperity, the city attracted traders and travelers from all over the world.

Today, Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, celebrated for its stunning ruins, temples, and rich cultural legacy.

Famous Monuments in Hampi

  • Virupaksha Temple: A sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, renowned for its intricate architecture.
  • Vittala Temple: Famous for the iconic stone chariot and the musical pillars.
  • Lotus Mahal: A unique palace with Indo-Islamic architectural elements.
  • Hazara Rama Temple: Known for its beautiful carvings depicting scenes from the Ramayana.
  • Hemakuta Hill: A cluster of ancient temples offering panoramic views of Hampi.

Visiting Information

The best time to visit Hampi is between **October and February**, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the ruins.

How to Reach:

  • **By Air:** Hubli Airport, about 150 km away, is the nearest airport.
  • **By Train:** Hospet Junction is the closest railway station, just 13 km from Hampi.
  • **By Road:** Well-connected by road from major cities like Bengaluru and Hyderabad.

Visitors can explore the ruins on foot, by cycle, or through guided tours. Hampi also offers options for trekking and coracle rides on the Tungabhadra River.
