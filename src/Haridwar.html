About Haridwar

Haridwar, located in the northern state of Uttarakhand, is one of the most sacred cities in India. Known as the 'Gateway to the Gods,' it is a major hub for Hindu pilgrims.


The city is famous for its ghats along the Ganges River, the evening Ganga Aarti, and the Kumbh Mela, which attracts millions of devotees and tourists from around the world.

Famous Places

Har Ki Pauri

The most famous ghat in Haridwar, known for its evening Ganga Aarti.

Mansa Devi Temple

A sacred temple dedicated to Goddess Mansa Devi, located atop the Bilwa Parvat.

Chandi Devi Temple

A popular temple dedicated to Goddess Chandi, situated on the Neel Parvat.
